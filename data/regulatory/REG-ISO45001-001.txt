REGULATORY REFERENCE: REG-ISO45001-001
Standard: ISO 45001:2018 — Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems
Clause: 4 — Context of the Organisation
Version: First Edition 2018-03
Issuing Body: International Organisation for Standardisation (ISO)

CLAUSE SUMMARY
Clause 4 requires an organisation to determine external and internal issues that affect its ability to achieve intended OH&S outcomes, to understand the needs and expectations of workers and other interested parties, and to determine the scope of the OH&S management system.

CLAUSE 4.1 — UNDERSTANDING THE ORGANISATION AND ITS CONTEXT
The organisation must determine external and internal issues that are relevant to its purpose and that affect its ability to achieve the intended outcome(s) of its OH&S management system. External issues include: legal, technological, competitive, market, cultural, social, and economic environments; external interested parties and their requirements; changes in and trends in the organisation's business context.
Internal issues include: governance, organisational structure, roles, and accountabilities; policies, objectives, and strategies; resources, knowledge, and competencies; worker culture; relationships, and perceptions and values of workers.

CLAUSE 4.2 — UNDERSTANDING THE NEEDS AND EXPECTATIONS OF WORKERS AND OTHER INTERESTED PARTIES
Workers and other interested parties relevant to the OH&S management system must be identified. Their relevant requirements (needs and expectations) must be determined. Which requirements have or could have legal or other requirements must be assessed.
Interested parties include: workers, worker representatives, contractors, regulatory authorities, customers, and communities adjacent to operations.

CLAUSE 4.3 — DETERMINING THE SCOPE OF THE OH&S MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
Scope must be determined considering: external and internal issues; legal and other requirements; planned and performed work activities. Scope documented and available. The scope must not be used to exclude activities with OH&S risks from the system.

CLAUSE 4.4 — OH&S MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
The organisation must establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve an OH&S management system — including the processes needed and their interactions — in accordance with this document.

REQUIREMENTS FOR CORRECTIVE ACTION
Non-conformities found during context review — e.g., failure to identify all interested parties, incomplete scope that excludes significant risk activities — must be corrected and the root cause addressed through the CAPA system. Systemic failures in context review indicate a management system weakness (root cause category: Management System Weakness).

APPLICABILITY TO CAPA SYSTEM
CAPAs arising from Clause 4 failures include: regulatory changes not captured in context review, resulting in compliance gaps; worker concerns not identified, leading to unmitigated hazards; scope exclusions that leave significant operations without OH&S controls.
