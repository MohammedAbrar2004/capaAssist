REGULATORY REFERENCE: REG-ISO45001-003
Standard: ISO 45001:2018 — Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems
Clause: 6 — Planning
Version: First Edition 2018-03
Issuing Body: International Organisation for Standardisation (ISO)

CLAUSE SUMMARY
Clause 6 requires the organisation to systematically identify hazards, assess risks and opportunities, and plan actions to address them. It also requires legal and other requirements to be identified, and OH&S objectives with plans to achieve them to be established.

CLAUSE 6.1 — ACTIONS TO ADDRESS RISKS AND OPPORTUNITIES

6.1.1 General: When planning the OH&S management system, the organisation must consider the context issues and interested-party requirements and determine the risks and opportunities that need to be addressed.

6.1.2 Hazard Identification and Assessment of Risks and Opportunities:
The organisation must establish, implement, and maintain processes for proactive, ongoing hazard identification. Hazard identification must consider: how work is organised, social factors, leadership, and culture; routine and non-routine activities and situations; past incidents; potential emergency situations; people (including contractors, visitors); changes (actual or proposed); physical work environment; workplace design; processes, procedures, and activities.
Risk assessment must determine existing controls; evaluate OH&S risks with those controls in place; determine whether additional controls are needed.
Opportunities for OH&S improvement must also be assessed.

6.1.3 Determination of Legal and Other Requirements:
Process to identify and access legal and other requirements applicable to hazards. Determine how they apply to the organisation. Keep up to date. Communicate relevant legal requirements to workers.

6.1.4 Planning Actions:
Plan actions to address risks, opportunities, and legal requirements. Integrate into OH&S management system processes. Evaluate effectiveness of actions.

CLAUSE 6.2 — OH&S OBJECTIVES AND PLANNING TO ACHIEVE THEM
OH&S objectives must: be consistent with OH&S policy; consider applicable requirements; consider the results of hazard and risk assessment; be measurable or capable of performance evaluation; be monitored; be communicated; be updated as appropriate. Document OH&S objectives. Plan: what will be done; what resources are required; who will be responsible; when it will be completed; how results will be evaluated.

REQUIREMENTS FOR CORRECTIVE ACTION
Incidents arising from hazards not identified in the risk assessment indicate a failure of the hazard identification process (root cause category: Missing Inspection or Management System Weakness). Root cause analysis must determine whether the gap was: methodology failure; scope exclusion; change not captured in MOC; non-routine activity not assessed. Corrective actions must address the process gap, not only the specific hazard.
