CLAUSE SUMMARY
This reference document combines ISO 14001:2015 Annex A interpretive guidance with a practical environmental legal compliance reference for AcerTech India operations. It supplements the clause-specific regulatory references with applicability guidance.

ANNEX A — KEY GUIDANCE POINTS

A.6.1.2 — Environmental Aspects: The term "environmental aspect" refers to elements of an organisation's activities, products, or services that interact with the environment. Not every aspect will be significant — significance is determined by the criteria the organisation establishes. Common errors: assessing only current state; not considering abnormal operations (start-up, shut-down, maintenance, spills); not considering life cycle impacts beyond the facility boundary.

A.8.1 — Operational Controls: ISO 14001 does not mandate a specific format for operational controls. They may be: documented procedures; equipment specifications; training requirements; inspection schedules; or any combination. The key test: would the absence of this control result in deviation from the environmental policy, objectives, or compliance obligations?

A.10.2 — Corrective Action Proportionality: Corrective actions must be proportionate to the significance of the environmental impact. A minor administrative non-compliance and a chemical spill reaching a waterway both require corrective action — but the depth of root cause analysis, the robustness of the corrective action, and the speed of implementation must differ accordingly.

APPLICABLE INDIAN ENVIRONMENTAL LEGISLATION (summary reference)
Environment (Protection) Act 1986 and Rules 1986: Framework legislation; empowers MoEFCC to issue standards and directions.
Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1974: Discharge standards for effluent to water bodies; consent to establish and operate from State Pollution Control Board (SPCB).
Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1981: Stack emission standards; consent to establish and operate from SPCB.
Hazardous and Other Wastes (Management and Transboundary Movement) Rules 2016: Identification, storage, labelling, transport, and disposal of hazardous waste; authorisation from SPCB.
Solid Wastes Management Rules 2016: Segregation, collection, and disposal of non-hazardous solid waste.
Chemical Accidents (Emergency Planning, Preparedness and Response) Rules 1996: Emergency planning requirements for facilities above threshold quantities of hazardous chemicals.

CONSENT CONDITIONS
Each AcerTech site with process operations holds Consent to Establish and Consent to Operate from the relevant SPCB. Conditions include: specific effluent discharge limits (pH, BOD, COD, suspended solids, heavy metals); stack emission limits (particulate, SO2, NOx); noise limits at site boundary; hazardous waste quantities and approved disposal routes. Consent conditions are site-specific documents held by the Site EHS Officer and Group EHS.
Exceedance of consent conditions must be reported to SPCB within the timeframe specified in the consent. Group EHS maintains the register of consent conditions and renewal dates.
