SOP ID: SOP-EHS-016
Title: Racking Inspection and Warehouse Storage Safety
Version: 2.0
Effective Date: 2023-08-01
Review Date: 2025-08-01
Owner: Warehouse Operations / EHS
Applicable Sites: Sites 2, 5, 10 (warehouses); all sites with racking installations
Regulatory Reference: ISO 45001:2018 Clause 8.1.2; SEMA Code of Practice for the Use of Static Racking; OSHA 29 CFR 1910.159

PURPOSE
To prevent racking collapse, falling objects, and structural failures in warehousing environments through regular inspection, damage control, and safe loading practice.

SCOPE
All pallet racking, cantilever racking, shelving, and mezzanine floors in AcerTech warehouses and storage areas.

RACKING INSPECTION PROGRAMME
User (daily, before use of each bay): Visual check for visible damage — bent or cracked uprights, displaced beams, missing beam locks, stored goods overhanging and creating instability. Report damage immediately to supervisor; do not load damaged bay.
Supervisor (weekly): Walk all racking aisles; check bay load labels; ensure no unauthorised modifications; confirm rack protection (end barriers, upright protectors) in place. Log on EHS-FORM-041.
Racking Inspector (SEMA/PRISM qualified, minimum annually): Full independent inspection. Green/Amber/Red classification:
  - Green: No damage, safe to use.
  - Amber: Damage requires monitoring or repair within defined timeframe; may remain in service with reduced load or restricted access to damaged bay.
  - Red: Immediate unloading; do not use until repaired and re-inspected.

DAMAGE CLASSIFICATION AND RESPONSE
Upright column damage:
  - Bow exceeding 3 mm in any 500 mm length: Amber minimum; Red if in load path.
  - Tear, crack, or perforation: Red immediately.
  - Base plate missing or anchor bolts missing: Red immediately.
Beam damage:
  - Permanent set (deflection exceeding 1/180 of beam span): Amber.
  - Cracked weld, missing safety clip: Red.
Damaged racking: Tag with "Do Not Use" (red tag). Unload bay. Notify Warehouse Supervisor and EHS Officer. Arrange repair by rack manufacturer or qualified racking engineer only.

SAFE LOADING RULES
1. Never exceed the Safe Working Load per bay, posted on the load notice at each rack entry.
2. Loads must be on suitable pallets (no broken blocks, no overhanging product).
3. Ensure beam locks are engaged after loading.
4. Do not store loose, unstable, or unsecured loads at height.
5. No picking or stacking at height from forklifts unless the forklift is rated and the operator trained for order picking.
6. Bottom aisle clear of obstruction for emergency exit at all times.

SEISMIC AND FLOOR LOADING CONSIDERATIONS (Sites in seismic zones)
Racking at Chennai (Site 1 and 9) and Hyderabad (Site 3) sites installed with seismic bracing per structural engineer specification. No modification to seismic bracing without Engineering sign-off.
Floor loading capacity confirmed before installation of new racking or addition of extra frames. Racking layout drawings approved by structural engineer on file in Engineering office.

POST-IMPACT PROCEDURE
Any forklift impact on racking: operator reports immediately (no exception). Supervisor inspects within 30 minutes. Affected bay tagged Red until inspection complete. Forklift damage report raised per SOP-EHS-006. CAPA raised if structural damage found.
