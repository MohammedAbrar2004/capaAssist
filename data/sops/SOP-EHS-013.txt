SOP ID: SOP-EHS-013
Title: Crane and Lifting Operations
Version: 2.7
Effective Date: 2024-03-01
Review Date: 2026-03-01
Owner: Group EHS / Engineering
Applicable Sites: Sites 1, 8, 9 (primarily); all sites with crane or overhead lifting equipment
Regulatory Reference: ISO 45001:2018 Clause 8.1.2; LOLER 1998; OSHA 29 CFR 1910.179

PURPOSE
To prevent death and injury from crane and lifting equipment failures, dropped loads, overloads, and collisions.

SCOPE
All lifting operations involving overhead cranes, mobile cranes, telehandlers, chain blocks, lever hoists, chain slings, wire rope slings, synthetic slings, and shackles. Includes routine production lifts and maintenance lifts.

DEFINITIONS
Appointed Person: Person with training and experience to plan lifting operations. Responsible for rigging method, load weight, and lift plan.
Crane Operator: Person operating the crane. Must be formally assessed and certified.
Banksman/Slinger: Person responsible for attaching the load and directing the crane operator. Must be trained and assessed.
Safe Working Load (SWL): Maximum load for which equipment is rated, marked on equipment.

COMPETENCE
Crane Operator: Formal lifting equipment operator training; site-specific familiarisation; operator card issued by Engineering Manager. Renewed every 3 years or following an incident.
Banksman/Slinger: OPITO-equivalent or in-house competency-based training. Renewed every 3 years.
Appointed Person: Minimum 5 years lifting experience plus formal Appointed Person course.

LIFT PLAN REQUIREMENTS (all non-routine and complex lifts)
A written lift plan is required for: lifts over 1 tonne; tandem lifts (two cranes); lifts near overhead power lines; lifts over occupied areas; lifts where load path is obscured; any crane or plant repositioning over a public area.
Lift plan contents: load weight (calculated, not estimated); rigging arrangement drawing; crane position and outrigger layout; exclusion zone; communication method; abort criteria.
Lift plan approved by Appointed Person and reviewed at lift site before work starts.

PRE-LIFT INSPECTION
Crane Operator pre-use check before each shift: rope/chain condition, hooks, limit switches, brakes, controls, emergency stop. Record on EHS-FORM-033.
Slings and lifting accessories: Inspect before each lift. Reject if: synthetic sling with cut fibres or chemical degradation; wire rope sling with more than 10% broken wires in any 10-diameter length or visible kinking; shackle with crack, bend, or missing pin.

STATUTORY INSPECTION
All lifting equipment: thorough examination by a competent person every 6 months (equipment used to lift persons: 6 months; all other lifting equipment: 12 months). Certificate retained at site for the life of the equipment.
Lifting accessories (slings, shackles): 6-monthly thorough examination. Certificate on file.

SAFE WORKING RULES
1. Never exceed SWL. De-rate for multiple slings based on sling angle (document in lift plan).
2. Never work under a suspended load. Exclusion zone enforced.
3. Banksman maintains line of sight with both load and operator at all times. Radio communication mandatory if direct line of sight is lost.
4. No persons to ride on hook, slings, or load unless a certified man-riding cage with safety factor 10:1 is used.
5. Land loads on adequate dunnage — never leave load suspended unattended.
6. Overhead power line exclusion: minimum 6 m horizontal clearance from 11 kV lines; consult engineering for higher voltages.
