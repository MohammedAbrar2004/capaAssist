SOP ID: SOP-EHS-024
Title: Emergency Response Planning and Business Continuity
Version: 2.0
Effective Date: 2024-06-01
Review Date: 2026-06-01
Owner: Group EHS / Site Manager
Applicable Sites: All sites
Regulatory Reference: ISO 45001:2018 Clause 8.2; ISO 14001:2015 Clause 8.2; OSHA 29 CFR 1910.38

PURPOSE
To ensure AcerTech sites are prepared to respond effectively to emergency situations, minimising harm to people, environment, and business continuity.

SCOPE
All foreseeable emergency scenarios at each AcerTech site. Each site must have a site-specific Emergency Response Plan (ERP) developed from this framework.

HAZARD IDENTIFICATION FOR EMERGENCY PLANNING
Each site identifies worst-case emergency scenarios from: major accident hazard (COMAH/PNGRB threshold quantity) analysis for chemical sites; flood, earthquake, and extreme weather relevant to site geography; neighbouring site hazards (Tier 2 COMAH, chemical plants); internal scenarios (major fire, structural collapse, multi-casualty incident, chemical release).

EMERGENCY RESPONSE PLAN CONTENTS (mandatory per site)
1. Site layout plan: Building layout, hazardous materials locations, emergency shutdown points, assembly areas.
2. Scenario-specific response cards: One page per scenario; actions in sequence; names and contact numbers.
3. Emergency contacts: Internal chain (EHS Officer → Site Manager → Group EHS Director); external (fire brigade, police, ambulance, hospital, environmental regulator, chemical emergency response line).
4. Roles and responsibilities: Fire Warden, First Aider, Chemical Response Team (chemical sites), Media Spokesperson.
5. First aid resources: First aiders per shift minimum (1 per 50 employees; 1 per 10 in chemical processing areas); first aid kit locations; defibrillator locations and last checked date.
6. Business continuity: Key equipment list; backup supply contacts; IT recovery contacts.
7. Regulatory notification: Who to call, when, and what to report for environmental releases, LTIs, dangerous occurrences.

EMERGENCY DRILL PROGRAMME
Fire evacuation: Full drill every 6 months (see SOP-EHS-009). Time to clear building recorded. Deficiencies raised as CAPA.
Chemical spill response: Drill annually at Sites 3 and 7; observe and assess the chemical response team.
First aid scenario: Tabletop exercise annually; practical first aid assessment every 3 years with external assessor.
Business continuity: Tabletop exercise annually at Group level.

FIRST AID PROVISION
First Aid at Work (FAW) trained persons identified per shift on site induction board and emergency contact list. First aid kits inspected monthly by nominated person; contents replaced immediately after use. Eye wash stations at all chemical handling locations; inspect and flush weekly.

COMMUNICATION DURING EMERGENCIES
External communication: Only Site Manager or nominated spokesperson speaks to media, regulatory authorities, or the public during an incident. All other employees direct external enquiries to the Site Manager.
Internal notification: Emergency notification cascade tested as part of each drill.

REVIEW AND UPDATE
ERP reviewed annually and after: any emergency or near-emergency event; significant change to site layout or hazardous materials; change in key personnel named in the plan.
