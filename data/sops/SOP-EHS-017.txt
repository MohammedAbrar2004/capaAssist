SOP ID: SOP-EHS-017
Title: Contractor Safety Management
Version: 3.1
Effective Date: 2024-05-01
Review Date: 2026-05-01
Owner: Group EHS / Procurement
Applicable Sites: All sites
Regulatory Reference: ISO 45001:2018 Clauses 8.1.4, 8.4; ISO 14001:2015 Clause 8.1

PURPOSE
To ensure contractors working at AcerTech sites are competent, informed of site hazards, and comply with AcerTech safety standards throughout their engagement.

SCOPE
All contractors, subcontractors, and specialist service providers performing work on any AcerTech site, regardless of duration. Includes maintenance contractors, construction contractors, cleaning contractors, IT service engineers, and inspection bodies.

CONTRACTOR APPROVAL PROCESS
Step 1 — Pre-Qualification: Before engagement. Procurement sends EHS pre-qualification questionnaire to contractor. Review: relevant safety certifications (ISO 45001 or equivalent), insurance certificates (minimum INR 5 crore public liability), incident rate for previous 3 years (target LTI rate below 1.0 per 100 employees), risk assessment and method statement capability.
Step 2 — Approval: EHS Officer reviews pre-qualification. Approved contractors added to Approved Contractor Register (maintained by Procurement). Register reviewed annually.
Step 3 — Work Scope Submission: Before work starts. Contractor submits risk assessment and method statement (RAMS) specific to the work. EHS Officer reviews and approves before work commences. RAMS must cover: task description, hazards, controls, emergency procedures, PPE requirements, and list of personnel.

SITE INDUCTION
All contractors receive site induction before entering operational areas. Maximum time between induction and work start: 24 hours (re-induction required if gap exceeds 12 months).
Induction covers: site rules, emergency procedures, permit-to-work system, site hazards relevant to work area, PPE requirements, incident reporting.
Induction register signed by contractor and kept by site EHS Officer for 2 years.

PERMIT TO WORK
Contractors performing high-risk work must hold a valid permit: Hot Work (SOP-EHS-002), Confined Space Entry (SOP-EHS-003), Electrical Isolation (SOP-EHS-011), or Working at Heights (SOP-EHS-004). No permit = no work. Permits checked by EHS Officer during random inspections (minimum 1 per day during active contractor work).

SUPERVISION AND MONITORING
Site EHS Officer conducts minimum daily monitoring of contractor activities. Inspects against RAMS. Issues improvement notice if minor non-compliance. Stops work and escorts off site if serious violation (no PPE, working without permit, bypassing lockout). Contractor non-compliance report raised and sent to contractor's management within 24 hours.

CONTRACTOR INCIDENT REPORTING
Any contractor incident (near miss, injury, damage) on AcerTech premises reported to Site EHS Officer within 30 minutes. Investigated under same standards as AcerTech employee incidents (SOP-EHS-007). Findings shared with contractor management. Repeated contractor incidents result in suspension from approved register.

PERFORMANCE REVIEW
Contractor safety performance reviewed annually: incident rate, permit compliance rate, inspection results, outstanding CAPAs. Poor performance results in probationary status or removal from approved register.
