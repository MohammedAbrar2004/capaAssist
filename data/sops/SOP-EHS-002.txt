SOP ID: SOP-EHS-002
Title: Permit to Work (PTW) — Hot Work
Version: 2.5
Effective Date: 2023-09-01
Review Date: 2025-09-01
Owner: Group EHS
Applicable Sites: All sites
Regulatory Reference: ISO 45001:2018 Clause 8.1.2; NFPA 51B

PURPOSE
To control ignition sources and combustible materials during welding, cutting, grinding, and other hot work operations to prevent fire or explosion.

SCOPE
All hot work activities including welding (MIG, TIG, MMA, plasma), cutting, grinding, brazing, soldering, and any other process that generates sparks, flame, or heat above 50°C in a non-designated hot work area.

RESPONSIBILITIES
- Permit Issuer (Site EHS Officer or delegated supervisor): Issues, approves, and closes the permit. Must be physically present for site inspection before issue.
- Permit Holder (Authorized Worker or Contractor Supervisor): Responsible for compliance with all permit conditions throughout work duration.
- Fire Watch: Stationed during work and for a minimum of 60 minutes after work completion. Must have a charged fire extinguisher and direct communication with permit issuer.

PERMIT VALIDITY
Hot work permits are valid for one shift only (maximum 12 hours). Work continuing into the next shift requires a new permit. Permits cannot be transferred between permit holders.

PROCEDURE
Step 1 — Request: Permit Holder submits PTW request to Permit Issuer at least 4 hours before planned start.
Step 2 — Site Inspection: Permit Issuer physically inspects the work location with Permit Holder. Check: combustibles within 11 m radius removed or shielded; floors swept clean; drains and openings covered; fire extinguishers checked (minimum 9 kg CO2 or dry powder); fire detection not impaired; adjacent spaces inspected.
Step 3 — Atmospheric Testing: If work is in or adjacent to a confined space, flammable atmosphere, or chemical processing area — test with a calibrated gas detector. LEL must be less than 10%. Continuous monitoring required if source cannot be isolated.
Step 4 — Issue Permit: Permit Issuer signs and dates the permit. Assign Fire Watch. Post completed permit at the work location.
Step 5 — Conduct Work: Permit Holder ensures all conditions are maintained. Fire Watch monitors continuously. Immediately suspend work if conditions change (gas alarm, fire event, unauthorized personnel entering exclusion zone).
Step 6 — Close Out: On work completion, Permit Holder notifies Permit Issuer. Permit Issuer conducts post-work inspection after minimum 60-minute fire watch period. Sign and date closure section. File permit for 12 months.

PROHIBITED LOCATIONS (require additional approval from Site Manager and Group EHS)
- Chemical processing areas where flammable vapour cannot be excluded.
- Within 3 m of any compressed gas cylinder storage.
- In areas where atmospheric testing confirms LEL above 0% and source cannot be isolated.

PRE-WORK CHECKLIST (Permit Issuer to complete and sign)
[ ] All combustibles removed or shielded within 11 m
[ ] Floors swept and drains covered
[ ] Gas test completed and LEL less than 10%
[ ] Fire extinguishers checked and positioned
[ ] Fire Watch assigned and briefed
[ ] Adjacent areas inspected and clear
[ ] Work area isolated from normal operations where practicable

RECORD-KEEPING
Completed permits filed in site EHS permit log for minimum 12 months. Monthly review by Site EHS Officer for compliance trends. Permit statistics included in monthly EHS report.
