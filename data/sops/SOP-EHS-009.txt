SOP ID: SOP-EHS-009
Title: Fire Prevention, Detection, and Emergency Evacuation
Version: 3.3
Effective Date: 2024-06-01
Review Date: 2026-06-01
Owner: Group EHS
Applicable Sites: All sites
Regulatory Reference: ISO 45001:2018 Clause 8.2; OSHA 29 CFR 1910.38; Fire Safety Order 2005

PURPOSE
To prevent fires through hazard control, ensure early detection and alarm, protect occupants through safe evacuation, and minimise property damage through first-response firefighting where safe to do so.

SCOPE
All AcerTech sites, all buildings, all employees, contractors, and visitors. Includes fire prevention controls, detection and alarm systems, evacuation procedures, and fire extinguisher use.

FIRE TRIANGLE CONTROLS
Heat sources: Hot work permit required; no open flame in non-designated areas; electrical inspections to schedule.
Fuel sources: Flammable liquid storage in approved flammable cabinets; daily housekeeping of combustible waste; no accumulation of cardboard, packing materials, or rags.
Oxygen: No oxygen enrichment in confined or enclosed spaces; no oxygen lines in process areas without engineering review.

FIRE DETECTION AND ALARM SYSTEMS
Type: Automatic detection (smoke, heat, or flame detectors) in all buildings. Manual call points at all exit routes.
Alarm tone: Continuous bell or sounder — EVACUATE. Intermittent — Alert only (investigation).
Testing: Weekly alarm test (Monday morning, 15 seconds). Six-monthly full evacuation drill. Annual fire alarm system service by certified contractor.
False alarms: All false alarms investigated within 24 hours. Three false alarms in 12 months triggers full system review.

EVACUATION PROCEDURE
On Alarm:
1. Stop work immediately. Switch off ignition sources if safe to do so without delay.
2. Leave by the nearest designated fire exit. Do not use lifts.
3. Close doors behind you (do not lock).
4. Proceed to designated muster point for your area (posted on emergency information board and evacuation plan).
5. Report to Assembly Warden. Do not re-enter until clearance given by Site EHS Officer or emergency services.

Assembly Wardens (minimum 1 per 20 persons per zone):
- Account for all persons in their zone using zone attendance list.
- Report zone clear or missing persons to Chief Warden within 5 minutes of alarm.
- Do not re-enter building to search — notify emergency services.

Fire and Emergency Coordinator (Chief Warden): Site EHS Officer or nominated deputy.
- Confirms all zones clear; liaises with emergency services on arrival.
- Issues all-clear.

FIRE EXTINGUISHER REQUIREMENTS
Location: Within 30 m of any point in an operational area; at each exit on every floor.
Types (matched to risk):
  - Class A (paper, wood, fabric): Water or foam extinguisher
  - Class B (flammable liquids): CO2 or dry powder
  - Class C (gas): CO2 only
  - Class D (metals): Dry sand or specialist powder
  - Electrical (Class E): CO2 only — never use water
Monthly inspection: Site EHS Officer checks charge indicator, pin, and tamper seal. Log on EHS-FORM-019.
Annual service: Certified contractor. 5-year extended service or replacement.

USE OF FIRE EXTINGUISHERS (PASS technique)
Only attempt if: fire is small (bin/waste-paper size); you are between the fire and an exit; you are trained; you will not delay evacuation.
Pull the pin. Aim at the base of the fire. Squeeze the handle. Sweep side to side.
If the fire does not respond in one sweep — leave immediately.

HOT WORK CONTROLS IN FIRE PREVENTION
All hot work requires a Hot Work Permit (see SOP-EHS-002). Fire Watch for minimum 60 minutes after hot work completion.
