SOP ID: SOP-EHS-006
Title: Forklift Truck Safe Operation and Pre-Use Inspection
Version: 4.1
Effective Date: 2024-04-01
Review Date: 2026-04-01
Owner: Group EHS
Applicable Sites: Sites 1, 2, 4, 5, 8, 10 (all sites with forklift operations)
Regulatory Reference: ISO 45001:2018 Clause 8.1; OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178; PUWER 1998

PURPOSE
To prevent forklift-related injuries and property damage by establishing operator competency requirements, pre-use inspection standards, and safe operating rules.

SCOPE
All powered industrial trucks: counterbalance forklifts, reach trucks, order pickers, pallet trucks (ride-on), and tow tractors operated at any AcerTech site.

OPERATOR REQUIREMENTS
Operators must: hold a current AcerTech forklift operator certificate (site-specific); be assessed as competent on the specific truck type; be minimum 18 years of age; not be under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or medication affecting alertness.
Operator certificates renewed every 3 years. Site Supervisor signs off familiarity with site-specific hazards (pedestrian routes, racking layout, floor load limits) before first operation at a new site.

PRE-USE INSPECTION (every shift, completed before operation)
Record on EHS-FORM-022 or digital equivalent. Do not operate the truck if any item is marked fail — report to Maintenance immediately.

Walk-Around Check:
[ ] Tyres — correct pressure (pneumatic), no cuts or flat spots (solid)
[ ] Forks — no cracks at heel, correct set, retaining pins present, tips level
[ ] Mast — no cracked welds, chains equal tension and properly lubricated
[ ] Overhead Guard — secure, no cracks or missing bolts
[ ] Seatbelt / Operator Restraint — functional and retracts correctly
[ ] Lights and Horn — operational
[ ] Fluid Levels (if applicable) — engine oil, coolant, hydraulic fluid, battery water
[ ] Battery / Fuel — sufficient charge/fuel for full shift; no electrolyte leaks on electric trucks

Operational Check (at low speed in clear area):
[ ] Steering — responsive, no excessive play
[ ] Brakes (service and parking) — effective
[ ] Lift / Tilt / Reach Functions — smooth, no hydraulic drift over 30 seconds
[ ] Horn — audible within 5 m in typical site noise

SAFE OPERATING RULES
1. Pedestrian priority: Stop and give way at all pedestrian crossings and blind corners.
2. Speed limit: Maximum 8 km/h in operating areas; 5 km/h near pedestrians.
3. Load stability: Carry loads low (150–300 mm above floor) when travelling. Never travel with load raised.
4. Racking: Approach racking at right angles. Ensure adequate aisle width before entering.
5. Slopes: Travel with load facing uphill on gradients above 5%. No cross-slope travel.
6. Capacity: Never exceed rated capacity on data plate. Reduce capacity for non-standard attachments.
7. Passengers: Never carry passengers on forks or any part of the truck not designed for passenger transport.
8. Refuelling/Charging: Refuel only at designated areas. Battery charging in designated ventilated charging bay only.
9. Parking: Apply parking brake; lower forks; turn off. Do not park blocking emergency exits or fire equipment.

PEDESTRIAN SEGREGATION REQUIREMENTS
All forklift operating areas must have marked pedestrian walkways (100 mm yellow paint or permanent barrier). Forklift and pedestrian routes must be segregated wherever practicable. Temporary shared-use areas require a traffic management plan signed off by the Site EHS Officer.

INCIDENT REPORTING
Any forklift incident (including near misses, racking strikes, dropped loads) reported to the supervisor within 30 minutes and logged in the EHS incident system. Forklift involved in a personal injury incident is taken out of service pending investigation.
