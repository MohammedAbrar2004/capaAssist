SOP ID: SOP-EHS-008
Title: Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Selection, Issue, and Use
Version: 3.5
Effective Date: 2024-01-01
Review Date: 2026-01-01
Owner: Group EHS
Applicable Sites: All sites
Regulatory Reference: ISO 45001:2018 Clause 8.1.2; PPE at Work Regulations 1992; OSHA 29 CFR 1910.132

PURPOSE
To ensure the correct PPE is selected, issued, fitted, maintained, and replaced to protect employees from residual hazards after elimination and engineering controls have been applied.

SCOPE
All employees, contractors, and visitors at AcerTech sites where PPE is required. PPE must be the last resort in the hierarchy of controls, not the first response to a hazard.

HIERARCHY OF CONTROLS REMINDER
PPE does not eliminate hazards. Supervisors must document that elimination, substitution, engineering, and administrative controls were considered before PPE is specified as the primary control.

PPE SELECTION PROCESS
Step 1: Identify hazard and exposure level from risk assessment.
Step 2: Determine required protection standard (CE category, ANSI rating, or equivalent).
Step 3: Select from the approved PPE register (maintained by Group EHS and updated annually).
Step 4: Conduct fit testing where required (RPE, hearing protection, eyewear).
Step 5: Issue with user training before first use.

MANDATORY SITE PPE (minimum standard for all persons entering operational areas)
- Safety footwear: S3 category (steel toe, steel midsole, ankle support)
- High-visibility vest or jacket: EN ISO 20471 Class 2 minimum (all sites with vehicle movements)
- Safety helmet: EN 397; replaced every 5 years or after impact

TASK-SPECIFIC PPE MATRIX
Eye/Face Protection:
  - Chemical splash risk: Indirect-vent goggles + face shield
  - Grinding/cutting: EN 166 safety spectacles with side shields; face shield for cut-off disc work
  - Welding: Auto-darkening welding helmet (minimum shade 10)

Respiratory Protection (RPE):
  - Dust (organic, mineral): P3 half-mask or powered air purifying respirator (PAPR); annual fit test
  - Chemical vapours: Gas filter half-mask — filter type matched to chemical per SDS; annual fit test
  - Confined space / unknown atmosphere: Self-contained breathing apparatus (SCBA) only; 6-monthly fit test
  - RPE requires fit test record and medical assessment for negative pressure respirators

Hand Protection:
  - Chemical handling: Nitrile minimum 0.3 mm; consult SDS for specific chemical compatibility
  - Cut risk: EN 388 cut level C or above for bladed operations
  - Heat/welding: EN 407 rated gloves; no synthetic gloves near ignition sources
  - Electrical (HV): Insulated rubber gloves with leather over-gloves, voltage-rated

Hearing Protection:
  - Above 80 dB(A): Protection available and recommended
  - Above 85 dB(A): Protection mandatory; minimum SNR 20
  - Above 100 dB(A): Double protection (ear muff over ear plug)

PPE INSPECTION AND REPLACEMENT
Pre-use inspection by user before each task. Reject if: damaged, degraded, expiry date passed, or filter saturation (RPE).
Detailed inspection by supervisor: monthly for high-risk PPE (SCBA, chemical gloves); quarterly for standard items.
Replace on request — no PPE is reused if integrity is in doubt.
Records: Issue, fit test, and inspection records maintained per employee in HR system.

VISITOR PPE
Site visitors must be provided with minimum site PPE before entering operational areas. Visitor PPE kit maintained at site reception. Visitor briefed on PPE requirement during site induction (maximum 5 minutes).

CONTRACTOR PPE
Contractors must provide their own PPE to the same or higher standard as AcerTech requirements. Spot-checks by Site EHS Officer; non-compliant contractors removed from site pending corrective action.
