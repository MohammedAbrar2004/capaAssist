SOP ID: SOP-EHS-015
Title: EHS Training Management and Competency Records
Version: 2.0
Effective Date: 2024-01-01
Review Date: 2026-01-01
Owner: HR / Group EHS
Applicable Sites: All sites
Regulatory Reference: ISO 45001:2018 Clauses 7.2, 7.3; ISO 14001:2015 Clause 7.2

PURPOSE
To ensure all employees are trained to the standard required for their role, training is verified as effective, and records are maintained to demonstrate competency.

SCOPE
All AcerTech employees and long-term contractors (6 months or more). Covers: induction training, role-specific EHS training, regulatory mandatory training, and refresher training.

TRAINING MATRIX
HR and Group EHS maintain a training matrix for each site, mapping job roles to required training modules. The matrix is reviewed annually and updated when: new regulations come into force; new hazards are introduced; incidents indicate a training gap; job roles change.
Training modules in the matrix carry: training method (classroom, on-the-job, e-learning); frequency (once-off, annual, triennial); assessment method (test, observation, competency check); minimum pass mark; and records retention period.

TRAINING TYPES
Induction Training (before starting work in operational areas):
- General site safety (2 hours minimum): Emergency procedures, first aid locations, fire exits, permit-to-work overview, hazard reporting, PPE requirements.
- Role-specific hazard briefing (supervisor-led): Hazards specific to the work area and role. Signed off by supervisor.
- Visitor and contractor induction (15–30 minutes): Fire exits, muster points, PPE, no-go areas.

Regulatory Mandatory Training (must hold current certificates):
- Forklift operation: Every 3 years
- Crane operation/slinging: Every 3 years
- Confined space entry/attendant: Every 3 years
- LOTO authorised employee: Every 3 years
- First Aid at Work: Every 3 years
- Fire Warden: Every 3 years
- SCBA / RPE use: Annual fit test + every 3 years operational training

Role-Specific Competency Assessment:
Certain roles require demonstrated competency, not just attendance:
- Permit Issuer: Written test (pass mark 80%) plus supervised issuance of 5 permits.
- Incident Investigator: Case study assessment scored by EHS Manager.
- EHS Auditor: ISO 45001 internal auditor course plus two witnessed audits.

TRAINING DELIVERY
Training providers: Internal trainers must be assessed as competent in training delivery. External providers approved by HR. Training records stored in HR system (individual file and site register).
Training effectiveness: Post-training assessment (test or observation) for all mandatory training. Assessment result recorded. Pass marks per module set in training matrix.
Re-training: Required if assessment not passed on first attempt. Second failure triggers supervisor review and revised training plan.

TRAINING GAP IDENTIFICATION
Training gaps identified by: training matrix compliance check (monthly — HR); incident investigation (where training gap is identified as a root cause); audit findings; return from absence greater than 6 months.
Training gaps classified as High (regulatory mandatory overdue) or Medium (role-specific overdue). High gaps must be closed within 30 days. Employee cannot work in that role unsupervised while gap exists.

RECORD MANAGEMENT
Training records retained: duration of employment plus 7 years minimum. Certificate copies held in employee file. Site training register maintained by HR and available to Site EHS Officer on request.
Training records are not deleted to hide gaps — gaps must be closed, not deleted.
