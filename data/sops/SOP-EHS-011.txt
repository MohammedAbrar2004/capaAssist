SOP ID: SOP-EHS-011
Title: Electrical Safety and Isolation Procedure
Version: 2.2
Effective Date: 2023-07-01
Review Date: 2025-07-01
Owner: Group EHS / Engineering
Applicable Sites: All sites
Regulatory Reference: ISO 45001:2018 Clause 8.1.2; OSHA 29 CFR 1910.333; Electricity at Work Regulations 1989

PURPOSE
To prevent electric shock, arc flash, burns, and fire from contact with live electrical conductors or from failure to confirm electrical isolation before maintenance work.

SCOPE
All work on or near electrical systems including: panel work, cable repairs, motor maintenance, instrumentation work, and any task that requires an electrical isolation. Applies to LV (below 1000 V AC) and HV (above 1000 V AC) systems. Both require isolation; HV requires additional precautions.

COMPETENCE REQUIREMENTS
No person shall work on electrical equipment unless they are an Electrically Authorised Person (EAP) for the voltage level involved.
LV Authorised Person: Qualified electrician (ITI / NTC / equivalent) with site-specific authorisation from the Engineering Manager. Renewed every 3 years.
HV Authorised Person: As above plus completed HV switching and safety course. HV work requires a second EAP present at all times.
Electrical Competent Person: May work under direct supervision of an EAP; cannot work unsupervised or issue isolations.

ELECTRICAL ISOLATION PROCEDURE (LV)
Step 1 — Identify: Identify all sources of supply to the equipment (mains, UPS, capacitor banks, back-EMF from motors).
Step 2 — Notify: Inform all affected production staff of planned isolation; coordinate with Operations Supervisor.
Step 3 — Isolate: Open the isolator, switch, or circuit breaker. For motor circuits, also isolate at the motor control centre (MCC) and local isolator.
Step 4 — Lock Out: Apply personal padlock to isolator in OFF position. Attach danger tag. See SOP-EHS-001.
Step 5 — Test for Dead: Using a calibrated proving unit, verify the tester is working (L to N gives indication). Test phase-to-phase, phase-to-neutral, and phase-to-earth. Confirm zero voltage. Test tester again to confirm it still works.
Step 6 — Discharge: Discharge capacitors and stored energy where present. Allow 5 minutes minimum for large drive capacitors to discharge before test.
Step 7 — Work: Proceed with electrical work. Maintain isolation throughout.
Step 8 — Restore: Check no tools or persons in equipment. Remove personal locks. Inform Operations Supervisor. Restore supply via normal switching sequence.

ARC FLASH AWARENESS
Before opening any live panel (for testing, fault diagnosis, or measurement), assess arc flash incident energy. For panels above 40 cal/cm2 incident energy — do not perform live work unless no alternative; requires HV-level precautions and Group EHS approval.
Minimum arc flash PPE for LV work: Arc flash face shield (minimum 8 cal/cm2 rating); arc flash gloves; fire-resistant (FR) coverall. Standard PPE is not sufficient.

TEST EQUIPMENT
Only CAT IV or CAT III rated test instruments to be used on LV systems. Test instrument calibration current, not expired. Damaged test leads not to be used.

PROHIBITED PRACTICES
- Working live unless formally approved and no dead-work alternative exists.
- Defeating safety interlocks or removing guards to permit access.
- Using non-rated or uncalibrated test instruments.
- Single-person working on HV systems.
- Working on LV systems during thunderstorm conditions.
