SOP ID: SOP-EHS-020
Title: Hazardous Substance Assessment (COSHH) and Control
Version: 2.5
Effective Date: 2024-02-01
Review Date: 2026-02-01
Owner: Group EHS / Chemical Handling Supervisor
Applicable Sites: Sites 3, 7 (primary); all sites using hazardous substances
Regulatory Reference: ISO 14001:2015 Clause 8.1; COSHH Regulations 2002; GHS/CLP Regulation; OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1200

PURPOSE
To prevent occupational ill-health from inhalation, skin contact, ingestion, or injection of hazardous substances by identifying hazards, assessing exposure, and implementing controls.

SCOPE
All substances used, produced, or arising from work activities that present a health hazard: chemicals, dust, fumes, vapours, biological agents, and process by-products. Includes cleaning products, maintenance lubricants, and waste streams.

CHEMICAL INVENTORY AND SDS MANAGEMENT
All sites maintain a chemical inventory: substance name, SDS issue date, GHS hazard classification, storage location, maximum quantity on site, and responsible custodian.
SDS must be: current version (check supplier website annually); available at the point of use in the working language of the site; and reviewed by EHS Officer before a new substance is introduced.
Substances without an SDS (unlabelled containers, unmarked waste) — quarantine and notify EHS Officer. Do not use or handle until SDS is obtained or substance is disposed of as hazardous waste.

COSHH ASSESSMENT PROCESS
Step 1 — Identify Substance and Form: Liquid, solid, dust, vapour, fume? Describe task and duration of exposure.
Step 2 — Hazard Classification: From SDS Section 2 (GHS classification). Note: carcinogen, mutagen, reproductive toxin (CMR) substances require enhanced controls regardless of quantity.
Step 3 — Who is Exposed: Job roles exposed, duration, frequency.
Step 4 — Current Controls: Engineering (LEV, containment), administrative (time limits, training), PPE (gloves, RPE).
Step 5 — Assess Adequacy: Are current controls likely to control exposure below the Workplace Exposure Limit (WEL) or, for CMRs, below the detection limit where technically achievable?
Step 6 — Specify Additional Controls: If current controls are inadequate — specify additional engineering controls first, administrative second, PPE last.
Step 7 — Approve and Record: EHS Officer signs COSHH assessment. Review annually and when substance, task, or quantity changes.

SUBSTITUTION REVIEW
Before accepting a new substance, EHS Officer evaluates whether a less hazardous substitute can perform the same function. Substitution decision documented. No CMR substance introduced without Group EHS Director approval and assessment of all available alternatives.

EXPOSURE MONITORING
Substances with WEL (see EH40 or ACGIH TLV list): Air sampling to verify controls are achieving exposure below 50% of WEL (target). Sampling by qualified industrial hygienist. Frequency: initially on introduction, then when process changes, and at minimum every 3 years for stable processes. Results in employee exposure records for 40 years.

LEV (LOCAL EXHAUST VENTILATION) INSPECTION
LEV systems inspecting annually by competent person. Inspection covers: face velocity at capture points; duct integrity; filter condition; fan performance. LEV examination report retained for 5 years. Any LEV deficiency triggers immediate use restriction until repair.
