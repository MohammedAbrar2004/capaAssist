SOP ID: SOP-EHS-007
Title: Incident Investigation and Root Cause Analysis
Version: 2.8
Effective Date: 2024-01-01
Review Date: 2026-01-01
Owner: Group EHS
Applicable Sites: All sites
Regulatory Reference: ISO 45001:2018 Clauses 9.1.2, 10.2; ISO 14001:2015 Clause 10.2

PURPOSE
To ensure all incidents and near misses are investigated systematically, root causes are identified, and effective corrective actions are implemented to prevent recurrence.

SCOPE
All workplace incidents: injuries (LTI, MTI, first aid), occupational illnesses, near misses, dangerous occurrences, property damage above INR 10,000, and environmental releases. Includes contractor incidents occurring on AcerTech premises.

DEFINITIONS
Lost Time Injury (LTI): Any work-related injury resulting in time away from work on any day after the day of injury.
Medical Treatment Injury (MTI): Injury requiring treatment beyond first aid (sutures, prescription medication, restricted duties).
Near Miss: An unplanned event that did not result in injury or damage but had the potential to do so.
Root Cause: The fundamental, systemic reason for an event that, if corrected, prevents recurrence of this and similar incidents.

INVESTIGATION TIMELINES
Near Miss and First Aid: Investigation within 48 hours. Report filed within 5 working days.
MTI and Property Damage: Investigation within 24 hours. Report filed within 5 working days. Preliminary report to Group EHS within 24 hours.
LTI and Dangerous Occurrence: Investigation begins within 4 hours. Lead investigator to be Site EHS Officer or above. Preliminary report to Group EHS within 4 hours. Full report within 7 working days.
Fatality or Multiple LTI: Investigation team led by Group EHS Director. Regulatory notification as required. Full report within 14 working days.

INVESTIGATION PROCEDURE
Step 1 — Scene Preservation: Preserve the scene. Photograph before anything is moved. Collect physical evidence (failed parts, samples). Identify witnesses.
Step 2 — Witness Interviews: Interview witnesses individually and as soon as practicable. Use open questions. Do not suggest answers. Record verbatim where possible.
Step 3 — Timeline Reconstruction: Build a chronological timeline of events leading to the incident. Identify deviations from expected process.
Step 4 — Root Cause Analysis Method: Select appropriate RCA method based on complexity:
  - Simple incidents: 5 Why Analysis (document each Why step)
  - Complex incidents: Fishbone / Ishikawa Diagram (6 M categories: Man, Machine, Method, Material, Measurement, Environment)
  - High-consequence or systemic incidents: Fault Tree Analysis or Bow-Tie
Step 5 — Contributing Factors: Identify all contributing factors. Separate immediate causes (unsafe acts/conditions) from underlying causes (management system gaps).
Step 6 — CAPA Development: Generate corrective and preventive actions targeting root causes and systemic gaps. Each action must have: owner, due date, evidence of completion, and effectiveness check method.
Step 7 — Report: Complete investigation report on EHS-FORM-011 (or CAPA AI system). Include: description, timeline, evidence, RCA analysis, contributing factors, failed controls, corrective actions.

QUALITY CHECKS (EHS Officer pre-submission review)
- Root cause statement does not end at "human error" without identifying the systemic gap.
- At least one preventive action addresses similar operations at other sites where applicable.
- Actions are specific (who, what, by when) — not generic ("train the team", "raise awareness").
- Effectiveness check method is defined and scheduled for each corrective action.

EFFECTIVENESS REVIEW
All corrective actions reviewed for effectiveness within 90 days of due date. Effectiveness result (Pass/Fail) recorded in CAPA system. Fail result triggers new investigation and updated CAPA.

REGULATORY REPORTING
Fatalities, specified injuries, and dangerous occurrences reported to relevant authority per jurisdiction requirements. Group EHS maintains regulatory notification register.
